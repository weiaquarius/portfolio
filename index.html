NCTU+

Internship website

The website was designed last summer and the first UX research project for me. We chose to design a better organized internship website since the students found difficulty to search the suitable intern information online.

I was part of the project to do the user research and UI prototypes design.

Design Background

Target Users

Junior/Senior/Master students looking for internship.

Research Propose

Create one organized internship website and help the students fast catch up the information they need.

The Challenge

It was the first time for me to run UX research project. It was hard for me to do the interviewing part because there are rules to follow, such as, no leading question, keep asking why and background reasons, avoid misleading our interviewees.

Solved:After practicing many times and learning to ask right questions, I felt a sense of accomplishment and enjoyed the process a lot.

Research Findings

"It is more efficient for the users to ask people than search online because they can get the prompt answer and direct instruction."

"Advertisements are effective to the users."

"Organized information is important to the users. For example, more often than not, information usually messy in startup and users find it hard to filter messages. The internship website needs great organized pattern."


Persona

Evonne is a junior college student who has no job experiences and doesn't know how to create personal resume and wants to ask professional experts or experienced people for help.

My
Role

Because it was the first workshop in NCTU+, all the group members ran the project together. And there are 3 people in our group.

I took part in interviewing, customer journey map, brain storming and Hi-Fi UI designs. In addition, we used Adobe XD as our prototyping tool.

Finally, we needed to present the prototypes to frond-end group in order to build up the websites. I was the only presenter and learned a lot from Q&A and feedback section from tech team.

HCI

Final Project

Messenger group redesign

This project was run last semester in HCI course.Our team wanted to go through research and design part. Because of time constraints, we chose to redesign the popular and mature product on market.

Next, we found our pain points on using messenger app. Therefore, we devoted ourselves to user research and application design.

I took part in user research, affinity diagram and prototype testing.

Design Background

Target Users

People using messenger. (But we mainly focused on students in National Tsing-Hua university).

Propose

1. We found that messages got messy easily in group. Therefore, we want to add some filter functions to help prioritize easily.

2. It is embarrassing to quit from the group, so we want to know users’ perspective and redesign the group function.

The Challenge

We didn’t specifically choose our topic and then failed to find the target users.At that time, we randomly chose the students and found out that they didn’t have the problem of leaving group but all had the problem of missing important messages in group.

Solved:We kept our previous data and started new research about the correspondent users who have problems of leaving the group. In other words, we ran two redesign parts in this HCI final project.

Research Findings

"Messages overlapping and information loss."

"Too many unused groups, feel pressured when leaving the group"

My
Role

I designed the interview script and went through parts of interviewing, affinity diagram. Mostly, the work was related to the qualitative study. Also, I did the prototype testing with printed version to the users’ and recorded their feedback.

NCTU+

Activities website

Our team have worked on this project since last semester. We had finished our qualitative study and would keep on research, design part.
NCTU+ team hope to revise the activities website and work hard to bring up an usable, informative website.

Design Background

Target Users

All NCTU students.

Propose

We want to understand the ideal pattern to publish activities and get related information for NCTU students.

Further:Figure out why NCTU+ activities website got low usage rate.

The Challenge

Some of our interviewee shared the experiences of snapping up concert tickets, which had few relevance to the NCTU+ website. The latter are mainly focus on the speed of the website. However, NCTU+ website mainly share information and organize what users’ favor. In other words, the types are different.

Solved: We filtered the messages when doing the affinity diagram. Even if some of the information showed non-related, we would double check the meaning and might use them in our ideation process, since some messages are imaginative and can take into consideration in design section.

Research Findings

"Images with detailed information catch users’ attention"

"Polling function and the analytical data it shows, help publisher to understand the participation patterns of participants."

My
Role

Interviewing, affinity diagram, user journey map and our next step will move on to set our scenario, persona and brain-storming.
