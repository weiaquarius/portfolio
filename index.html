Design Background


This is the final project from the course Software Studio, which I took when I was a junior.

Problem

Our group noticed that students and teachers find it difficult to find matched partners through tutor websites.

    ■ Paid websites

   1. They are too expensive for users.
   2. There are risks that users still cannot find suitable partners.

    ■ Free websites

   They are low-efficient and waste time.


Hence, our group decided to build one free tutor website to solve the users’ problem.

Target Users

People who want to use tutor websites to find teachers or students.

Research Propose

Our group wanted to design one free and efficient tutor website to help users find matching easily and quickly.

Persona

Research Findings

"I have limited information on the ambiguous matching process."

  ■ People don’t know each other too much because the matching process is conducted by the agency.
  ■ Information asymmetry. Matching progress is vague and people cannot track the timeline.

"Paid websites would decrease my desire to use the website, I don’t want to pay a lot in the process. "

  ■ People would decide not to use the paid website that costs a lot.
  ■ They can choose whether to pay or not for the usage of the function.

Final Solution

"Designed the recommend function to save users’ time and enhance efficiency."

  ■ Based on users’ preference for the region, wages, and subjects to recommend the matched cases.

The Challenge

1. How to highlight our key value among any other tutor website.

2. How to convey the sense of UX methodology to the front-end and back-end engineer. (For personal challenge)

Solved:
   1. After interviewing and user testing, we built up two features, a suitable matching page, and a matching progress page, to help users efficiently find their matched ones.

   2. Be empathetic: Since one of our team members had difficulties using tutor websites, I tried to use his usage experience to explain the meaning and working process of user research to him. After elucidation, all of my team members saw the significance of user experience we discussed the methodologies during the project.

My
Role

I am involved in user studying, UI design, and user testing.

That was the first time for me to design the whole page and also have the chance to test our users by using prototypes during the project.

Although there are 4 engineering students in our group, I am the one more like the product manager to use my knowledge about products, users, and technique to explain and work with them.

This project was running last semester in the HCI course. Our team wanted to go through the research and design part. Because of time constraints, we chose to redesign the popular and mature product on the market.

Next, we found our pain points on using the messenger app. Therefore, we devoted ourselves to user research and application design.

I took part in user research, affinity diagram and prototype testing.

Design Background

Target Users

People using messenger.

(But we mainly focused on students in National Tsing-Hua University).

Propose

1. We found that messages were messy in a group. Therefore, we want to take some user study and interview to know their experiences and feelings.

2. It is embarrassing to quit the group, so we want to know users’ perspectives and then redesign the group function.

Research Findings

"Messages overlapping and information loss in groups."

"Because of many unused groups, I feel pressured when leaving the groups."

My
Role

I designed the interview script and went through parts of the interviewing, the affinity diagram. Mostly, I applied qualitative approaches. Also, I did the prototype testing with a printed version to the users’ and recorded their feedback.

The Challenge

We didn’t choose our topic specifically and fail to find the target users. We randomly chose the students and found out that they didn’t have the problem of leaving groups but would feel difficulty using group features.

Solved:
   Created two persona, usage scenarios, and respectively addressed their problems.

   We kept our previous data of users feeling annoyed with messy messages in groups. At the same time, we started new research on the correspondent users who have problems leaving the group. Eventually, we addressed two problems from two different target audiences.
